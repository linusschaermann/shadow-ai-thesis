\subsection{Purpose} \label{purpose}

Planning - Explicit + Comprehensive + Reproducible

Purpose of this review is to identify and analyze existing literature on the phenomenon of \ac{SIT} and further the development of \ac{SAI} in organizational contexts. The \ac{SLR} aims to synthesize insights on the motivations behind \ac{SIT} and \ac{SAI}, their organizational consequences, and the governance approaches employed to manage these phenomena.

\subsection{Literature Search} \label{literature-search}

The literature search was conducted using four search strings across four databases
(AISeL, EBSCO, IEEE, and Web of Science), employing advanced search with Boolean
operators (\texttt{OR} and \texttt{AND}) applied to all fields or
title/abstract/keywords depending on the database.

\begin{table}[ht]

\toprule
\textbf{String} & \textbf{Purpose} & \textbf{Query} \\
\midrule
S1 & Shadow IT Baseline &
  \texttt{"shadow IT" OR "rogue IT" OR "IT workaround" OR "feral system"} \\
\addlinespace
S2 & Shadow AI Baseline &
  \texttt{"shadow AI" OR "unauthorized AI" OR "unsanctioned AI" OR "BYOAI"} \\
\addlinespace
S3.1 & Shadow IT $\times$ Shadow AI &
  \texttt{("shadow IT" OR "rogue IT" OR "IT workaround" OR "feral system") AND ("shadow AI" OR "unauthorized AI" OR "unsanctioned AI" OR "BYOAI")} \\
\addlinespace
S3.2 & Shadow IT $\times$ AI (broader) &
  \texttt{"shadow IT" AND "artificial intelligence"} \\

String~S3.2 was included to capture literature that may not explicitly use the
term \emph{Shadow AI} but discusses the intersection of Shadow IT and AI
technologies.
